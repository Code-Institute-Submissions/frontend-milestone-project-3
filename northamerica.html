north america

a - new york

The Subway is the best way to get around due to the traffic. Make sure you dont trap yourself in Manhattan, New York has so much more to offer. There are over 50 free tours so that is really good way of getting your bearings.

b - washingotn dc

The Capital of the US. See the White House, Capitol Building and Lincoln Memorial just by walking the city. A city full of history and lots of museums worth checking out.

c - chicago

Chicago is a beautiful city located right on Lake Michigan, a great place to visit but be warned in the winter it gets VERY cold. The city can be explored completely by foot given the time, walking along the river gives stunning views of downtown.

d - grand canyon

An absolutely incredible natural landmark. Great views from the top and if you time your trip right you get down into the valley where there are several beautiful hiking trails.

e - las vegas

To save a few $$$ stay off the main strip. There is a ton of hotels just a few blocks off and easily within walking distance. Bellagio fountain display is something you should see. And be aware of drinks prices in the casinos.

f - los angeles

If you do take the advice and stay out by Venice Beach head down Abbot Kinney Blvd. Restaurants, cafes, bars and shops. Everything you could need and all top quality! Head up the Hollywood hills and get a hike in, fantastic views of the city from there.

hot tips

1 - DON'T sleep on Chicago. An excellent and exciting city! Take your time an explore the city along the waterfront. And check out The Bean, a very odd but interesting structure.

2 - Grand Canyon. The helicopter tour is outstanding. But if you're strapped for cash, Pink Jeep tours offers a great alternative and will get you down to some stunning waterfalls in the heart of the valley.

3 - LA, rent a car! The lack of public transport and sheer size of the city makes it difficult or expensive to see the city. Samesum Hostel right by Venice Beach, great location.
